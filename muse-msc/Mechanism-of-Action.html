Step-by-Step Process

The Journey of a Muse Cell

From the moment of administration to complete tissue integration, follow the precise mechanism that makes Muse cell therapy revolutionary.

01

Circulation

Following simple intravenous injection, Muse cells enter the peripheral blood stream. Due to their uniquely small size (~13μm), they easily bypass the pulmonary trap, circulating freely throughout the body.

02

Detection & Homing

Damaged tissues emit Sphingosine-1-Phosphate (S1P) distress signals. Muse cells possess specialized receptors that detect this gradient, allowing them to actively migrate directly to the site of injury.

03

Phagocytosis

Upon arrival at the damaged tissue, Muse cells exhibit a unique capability: they phagocytose (engulf and clear) dead or dying cells. This critical step prepares the microenvironment for regeneration.

04

Differentiation

Finally, guided by the local microenvironmental cues of the cleared tissue, Muse cells differentiate into the specific cell types required, structurally integrating to restore functional tissue.

Beyond Direct Integration

Powerful Bystander Effects

While structural integration is their primary function, Muse cells also exert profound paracrine effects, fundamentally altering the local tissue microenvironment to promote healing.

Immunomodulation

Muse cells secrete powerful anti-inflammatory cytokines, calming hyperactive immune responses at the injury site and preventing further inflammatory tissue damage.

Angiogenesis

By releasing Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor (VEGF) and other factors, they stimulate the formation of new blood vessels, restoring critical blood supply to ischemic tissues.

Anti-Apoptosis

They provide trophic support to surrounding stressed cells, halting the cascade of programmed cell death (apoptosis) and preserving existing healthy tissue architecture.
