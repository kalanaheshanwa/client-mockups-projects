The Story

Built for complexity.
Driven by story.

I came to production through the floor — not the office. My early years were spent learning how sets actually work: the rhythm of a shooting day, the way departments communicate under pressure, the moments where a production either holds together or starts to unravel. That experience shaped everything about how I produce now.

I've worked across feature films, branded documentaries, content series, and docu-series — for Netflix, Prime Video, Amazon, YouTube Originals, and BET. The projects have ranged from intimate single-location shoots to large-scale international productions involving multiple countries, languages, and cultural contexts. Every one of them has taught me something I carry into the next.

What makes me different from most producers is that I've done the 1st AD job at a high level too. I know what it costs — in time, money, and morale — when the floor isn't run well. That understanding is baked into every production plan I build and every set I run. I protect the creative because I understand the operational, and vice versa.

I'm based in Los Angeles and work internationally. The projects I take on are chosen carefully — I'd rather do fewer things exceptionally well than spread thin across work that doesn't challenge me. If you're building something that requires both creative integrity and operational discipline, that's the conversation I want to have.

What I Believe

Three things that don't change,
regardless of the project.

01
Story First
The story is the brief. Every other decision flows from it.

I've never met a production problem that couldn't be traced back to a story problem that went unaddressed. I start every project by getting the creative clear — then I build the road to get there.

02
People Always
The crew makes the film. How you treat them shows in the work.

Sets where people feel respected and heard produce better work. That's not a philosophy — it's an observation I've made across hundreds of shooting days. How a set feels is a leadership choice, and it's one I take seriously.

03
Honest Always
Comfortable reassurance costs more than honest conversation.

I'll always tell you what I actually think — about the schedule, the budget, the creative, the risks. The productions that go wrong usually had someone who knew something was wrong and didn't say it early enough.

International Experience

Three continents. Dozens of locations.

I've produced and directed on location across Africa, Europe, and North America — navigating different regulatory environments, cultural contexts, crew structures, and logistical realities. That breadth shapes how I approach every production, wherever it happens to be set.

Industry Background

Floor to boardroom. Both directions.

My career started on set and grew through every layer of production — from PA to 1st AD to Producer to Executive Producer. I understand what every department actually needs because I've worked alongside most of them. That context makes me a better partner at every level of a production.

What Drives the Work

Stories that earn their audience.

The projects I'm most proud of are the ones where the story was hard to tell and we told it right — with honesty, with care, and without cutting the corners that mattered. That's the standard I hold every project to, whether it's a Netflix feature or a branded campaign for a global brand.
